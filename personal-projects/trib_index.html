Agatha Christie

The Queen of Crime

A breif timeline of Christie's life, along with her works.

  • Sep 15th, 1890 - Agatha Christie is born.
  • 1900 - Writes her frist poem.
  • 1905 - Sent to school in Paris.
  • 1914 - WWI begins. Joins the Voluntary Aid Detachment. Marries Archibald (Archie) Christie.
  • 1919 - Gives birth to daughter Rosalind.
  • 1920 - The Mysterious Affair at Styles
  • 1922 - The Secret Adversary
  • 1923 - Murder on the Links
  • 1926 - Disappears from home for 11 days. Causes massive upraor before she is recognized at a spa. Divorces her husband after he reveals his affair.
  • 1930 - Marries archeologist Max Mallowan.
  • 1934 - Murder on the Orient Express
  • 1937 - Murder on the Nile
  • 1939 - And Then There Were None
  • 1948 - The Rose and the Yew Tree
  • 1953 - After the Funeral
  • 1956 - Named Commander of the OBE(Order of the British Empire).
  • 1957 - Becomes the President of the Detective Club.
  • 1961 - The Pale Horse
  • 1967 - Endless Night
  • 1971 - Promoted to DBE(Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire).
  • Jan 12th, 1976 - Passes away in her home in Winterbrook.

You might recognize the names Herule Poirot and Miss Marple, Christie's two most famous detectives. But those characters weren't all. This timeline only offers a very shortlist of Agatha Christie's works. In her lifetime she wrote 66 detective novels, 14 short stories, and the world's longest-running play, The Mousetrap.
